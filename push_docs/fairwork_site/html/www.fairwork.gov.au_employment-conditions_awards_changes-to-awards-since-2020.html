Changes to awards since 2020

The Fair Work Commission (the Commission) started reviewing all modern awards in 2014, as part of its 4-yearly review of modern awards. During this process, the Commission has clarified how some award clauses work and changed other clauses.

In September 2019, the Commission confirmed it was finalising its review . Most of the updated awards started taking effect at different stages throughout 2020, although some started taking effect in 2021 or 2022. A few awards are yet to be updated. The updated awards have 2020 in their title.

What you should do

You can find the latest version of your award on our List of awards page.

See Start dates for updated awards for details on when each award was or will be updated.

If you've signed up for our email updates , we'll send you information about any significant changes to your award.

Employers need to make sure the latest version of the award (or awards if more than one applies to the business) is easily available to their staff. This can be by providing a digital version or a hard copy (for example, in a prominent place in the tea room or on a noticeboard at work).

What’s changed

For most awards, the biggest changes have been to the layout and language and the inclusion of rates tables to help you find the right pay rate. This means that your award should be simpler and easier to read and understand. The rules and entitlements in your award probably haven't changed significantly.

The Commission has made changes to awards over the last few years. During this time, we've updated our website and tools, and sent emails to subscribers about significant changes.

Changes to award coverage for the vehicle manufacturing industry

If you work or have a business in the vehicle manufacturing industry and you were previously covered by the Vehicle Award, you are now covered by the Manufacturing Award. From 29 May 2020, the manufacturing stream of the Vehicle Manufacturing, Repair, Service and Retail Award [MA000089] moved into the Manufacturing and Associated Industries and Occupations Award [MA000010].

The Vehicle Award's full title changed to the Vehicle Repair, Service and Retail Award [MA000089]. Find updated information in our Pay and Conditions Tool . The Manufacturing Award's name has remained the same, but the award now includes the vehicle manufacturing industry in its coverage clause.

Merging of the Quarrying Award and the Cement and Lime Award

From 4 February 2020, all of the Quarrying Award’s terms and conditions moved into the Cement and Lime Award, and it is now called the Cement, Lime and Quarrying Award [MA000055]. Find updated information in our Pay and Conditions Tool .

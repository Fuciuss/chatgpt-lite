Cement, Lime and Quarrying Award [MA000055]

Who the Cement, Lime and Quarrying Award covers

The Cement, Lime and Quarrying Award covers employers in the cement and lime industry and quarrying industry. Employees are covered if the award has a classification for the work they do.

The cement and lime industry means work with or related to receiving, manufacturing, handling or distributing of:

  • cement
  • clinker
  • flyash
  • lime and/or
  • hydrated lime

in or out of production establishments or bulk silos.

The quarrying industry means:

  • operations in:
    • lime and / or stone quarries
    • sand pits
    • gravel pits
  • operations (other than in a quarry) where the plant and equipment is mainly used to crush, screen and / or blend materials such as stone, brick, concrete, masonry, asphalt etc. to produce recycled material including aggregates, road bases, gravels, fine and coarse sands, or a blend of these.

Who the Cement, Lime and Quarrying Award doesn’t cover

The following employers and employees in the cement and lime industry are not covered by the Cement, Lime and Quarrying Award:

  • manufacturers of concrete products
  • manufacturers of cement products.

The following employers and employees in the quarrying industry are not covered by this award:

  • those who operate in dimension stone, brick, shale or slate quarries
  • clerical and administrative employees.

The Cement, Lime and Quarrying Award also doesn’t apply to employers and employees in the on-site building, engineering and civil construction industry who are covered by the Building and Construction Award.

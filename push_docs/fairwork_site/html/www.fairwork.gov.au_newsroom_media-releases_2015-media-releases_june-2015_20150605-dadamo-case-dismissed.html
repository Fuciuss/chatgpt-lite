Court dismisses legal action

5 June 2015

The Federal Circuit Court has dismissed the Fair Work Ombudsman’s legal action against a Perth electrical business.

In 2010, the Fair Work Ombudsman commenced legal action D’Adamo Nominees, which trades as the Wangara-based business L&A Electrics.

It was alleged that L&A Electrics underpaid an 18-year-old employee $9892.

The Court found that the employee was not an apprentice, because his apprenticeship agreement was not signed by a parent or guardian as required under the Industrial Training Act 1975 .

The Court also dismissed the Fair Work Ombudsman’s submission that the Electrical Contracting Industry Award 1978 applied to the employee on the basis of a factual finding that the employee did not fit within the classification of “electrical assistant” under the Award.

Cairns restaurant faces court for allegedly underpaying overseas worker $33,000

17 February 2016

The operators of a restaurant in Cairns are facing Court for allegedly short-changing an overseas worker more than $33,000 over a period of almost three years.

The Fair Work Ombudsman has commenced legal proceedings against Poisson Pty Ltd and Cairns businesswoman Miyuki Yogo.

They operate the Mature Yogo restaurant at ‘The Pier’ precinct, on Pierpoint Road.

The allegedly underpaid a 34-year-old female employee from Japan who was employed as a waitress and later as a manager.

The employee was sponsored by Poisson on a 187 Regional Sponsored Migration Visa for part of her employment.

She was allegedly underpaid her minimum hourly rate, casual loadings, shift allowances, annual leave entitlements and penalty rates for weekend, night and public holiday work between May, 2012 and April, 2015.

This allegedly resulted in a total underpayment of $33,693.

The employer’s obligations relating to issuing of pay-slips and record-keeping were also allegedly breached.

Fair Work inspectors investigated after the employee lodged a request for assistance.

Fair Work Ombudsman Natalie James says that while all outstanding entitlements have now been repaid, legal action is proceeding on the basis that the underpayment was significant and involved a vulnerable overseas worker.

Ms Yogo faces maximum penalties of up to $10,200 per contravention and Poisson Pty Ltd faces penalties of up to $51,000 per contravention.
